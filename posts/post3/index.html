Infinite Primes from 300BC

Himanshu, Nov 11 2020

One of the most classical proofs in Mathematics "There are INF Primes" proved by a method Mathematicians love Proof by Contradiction. So, let's just start with it without wasting no time.

STEP 1

Let's assume that there are finite primes [n]. So the list of Primes will look like [2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 ... Pn], where Pn is the Nth Prime.

STEP 2

NOTE We know that all numbers are made up of prime numbers, and if a number is not made up primes then itself a Prime.

55 = 5 x 11

1024 = 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 2

89 = 89 x 1

STEP 3

Coming back to our list of finite primes, now what if we multiply all these numbers together ?

2 x 3 x 5 x 7 x 11 x ... x Pn = Q

So Q will be divisible by every number from the list of finite primes. --- [I]

Also, Q > largest([2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 ... Pn])

STEP 4

Now, Add 1 to Q
Q' = Q + 1

STEP 5

Now, you know from STEP 3 [I]. That Q' will always leave a remainder of 1. When divided by any number from the list of finite prime.

Concluding that Q' is a prime greater than the greatest prime Pn.
Making our initial assumtion false. Hence Voila there are infinte primes!
